Requirement: Description of what system under development (SUD) should do and any constraints under which it must do it.
  • Serves as contract between development team and customer.
  • Focuses on what system will do not how will do it.
  • Usually written as a text document: cd. IEEE Standard 830.
  • Set of SHALL statements e.g. system shall do x and y.
  • Every requirement should be testable, always have some sort of metric - otherwise it’s not a requirement.

Requirements - something product must do, or quality product must have.
Functional
What the system must do and how it will be implemented e.g. function and data requirements. 
Non-functional
Quality that system must have, concerns the way the system functionality operates e.g. availability, performance, security, reliability.

Requirements Engineering:Systematic process of understanding the problem and specifying the solution. Once the solution has been validated, we need to manage the reqs.

Requirement Analysis Problems
  1. Stakeholders don’t know what they really want.
  2. Stakeholders express requirements in their own terms.
  3. Different stakeholders may have conflicting requirements.
  4. Organisational and political factors may influence the system requirements.
  5. Requirements change during analysis process. New stakeholders may emerge and the business environment may change.
