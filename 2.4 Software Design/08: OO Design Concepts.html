Modular Decomposition: Another structural level where sub-systems are decomposed into modules e.g. objects, data flows, components, services, aspects.




Objects
State: Set of attribute values and the links object may have with other objects.

Behaviour: Defined by what it does in response to receiving request from another object.

Identity: Each object has unique ID, even if state is identical to another object.
Classes
Description of group of objects with common attributes, behaviour, links and semantics. It is an abstraction in that it emphasises relevant characteristics and suppresses other characteristics.

Attribute: Property of class - describes range of values that property may hold in objects of that class.

Operation: Method or function that can be performance by an instance of a class.

Inheritance: Subclasses inherit all attributes and operations of their superclasses but are also able to define additional attributes and operations.


Relationships
Association: Objects typically related to one another in some fashion, e.g. student is in association with a course and degree.

Aggregation: Special form of association that connotes a ‘whole/part’ relationship.

Composition: Form of aggregation involves strong relationship between aggregate object and its component objects.
