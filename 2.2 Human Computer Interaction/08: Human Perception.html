Miller’s Law: Average person only keeps 7±2 pieces of information in working memory.

Representations
  • Perception extends our working memory to our environment, and we work with external representation on our goals.
  • Users form mental model of UI based on their perception of it.
  • Users seek information quickly with as little effort as possible.


Selective Attention
Divided Attention
Concentrating on one aspect or task - focus on one thing in many competing stimuli, but limits ability to track all events.
Splitting attention between multiple stimuli - switching between tasks rather than simultaneously attending.

Directing attention
  • Exogenous: Features that draw our attention - pops out.
  • Endogenous: Features of relevance to our task.
Inattention blindness
Change Blindness
Missing something even if it’s right in front of you because attention is elsewhere - focus on screen (tunnel vision).
Failure to notice difference between previous and current state - overlook alterations of UI.


Perception Bias
Experience

Gestalt principles (limitations)
  1. Proximity: Close together elements perceived to form a single group.
  2. Similarity: Similar shape, size colour elements, perceived as to belong together.
  3. Closure: If shape is not complete or space not enclosed and enough elements are present, we will perceive them as whole figure anyway.
  4. Continuity: If object forms continuation of another object, beyond ending points, we perceive pieces as parts of whole object.
  5. Figure and Ground: Our perception tends to separate from its background based on visual attributes e.g. contrast, colour, size.
