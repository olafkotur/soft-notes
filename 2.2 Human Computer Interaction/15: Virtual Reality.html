Extended Reality (XR)
  1. Virtual Reality (VR): Fully virtual environment
  2. Augmented Reality (AR): Overlay of virtual content, no interaction with physical environment.
  3. Mixed Reality (MR): Virtual and physical objects co-exist and interact.

Key aspects
  • Fully virtual world: Visual perspective responsive to movements.
  • Immersive: Perception of being physically present in virtual world. 
  • Sensory feedback: Requires many of our senses to be stimulated.
  • Interactivity: Crucial aspect of immersion.

VR Design Challenges
Large screen space
Regular screens usually take up 40 degrees, VR tends to take 110 degrees which is still smaller than our normal visual field of 190 degrees.
Predicting Behaviour
Difficult to predict user behaviour as they are given a 360 degree field of regard.
Depth
Occlusion - objects may be obstructed by closer objects. When this is not the case it causes strain and discomfort.
Restricted movements
Often not enough space in the real world to move around freely.
Cybersickness
Can occur during or after VR exposure, currently don’t know what causes this, nor a good enough preventing scheme.
