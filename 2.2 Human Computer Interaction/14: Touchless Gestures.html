Gesture Types
  • Pointing: Indicate objects and directions.
  • Semaphoric: Convey specific meanings based on commonly known vocabulary e.g. Thumbs up.
  • Iconic: Paths or shapes.

Cursor-based Interaction
  • Ray Casting: Pointing exactly where you want cursor to be.
  • Relative Pointing: Move things around freely.

Motion Correlation: Mimicking the movement of on-screen target, selection determined when user movement matches with displayed motion. Utilises smooth pursuit eye movements.
  • Scale invariant - size is irrelevant. 
  • No need to remember gestures
  • Reduces Midas touch problem.
  • Gestures can be re-used.

Gaze Awareness: We look at what’s relevant to us, gazing signals interest. 
  • Gaze Selection - ~400ms to confirm object selection.
  • Eye movements can be deliberate, show users content based on where they are looking.
