Visual Structure Example


Foveal vision
  • Concentrate visual processing in small area to extract detail - fovea covers 1% of the visual field but collects 50% of the visual data.
  • Moves eyes to align with object of interest for sufficient time (fixation -200ms).


Pre-attentive features: Detect without cognitive effort i.e. without focusing on object (pre-attentive processing) e.g. spotting red card in deck of blue cards. Use these techniques for good data visualisation.
Colour
Colour stands out from the rest e.g. red circle in group of blue circles.
FormShape stands out from the rest - not as strong as colour. Different types of form exist e.g. spatial grouping, size, length, width, shapes.
Movement
Flicker or motion are powerful in drawing attention in system warnings and tackles change-blindness. However, can become annoying so use with caution.
Positioning
Reference system (scape, map coords), conveys quantitative data at a glance e.g. volume sliders, GPS position, speedometer.


Graphical Perception: Ability of viewers to interpret visual encodings of data and thereby decode information in graphs. Some are less effective than others e.g. Adelson checkerboard.


Colour encoding
  • Human vision is optimised for edge contrast but not brightness, thus limitations to representations of data by colours exist.
  • Colour is not perceived as ordered, however, brightness is. We can display ordinal data by using colour differentiation - not particularly good for continuous data.
  • Colour vision deficiency (CVD) - Some people will perceive colours differently thus must ensure that encoding is not just done via colour.
