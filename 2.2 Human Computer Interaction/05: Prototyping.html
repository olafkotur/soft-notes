Prototype: Concrete manifestation of design idea created rapidly to test an idea, can be anything from paper-based to fully functioning software / hardware.
  • Allow us to explore and predict things in advance in an easy way with easy feedback.
  • Easy communication between stakeholders.
  • Low fidelity thus they are not digital and not interactive. 

Principles of prototyping
  • Fundamental: Create a manifestation that filters the quality in which designers and stakeholders are interested at given stage.
  • Economic principle: Make possibilities and limitations of idea visible in the simplest and most efficient way. 
Properties of Prototypes
Resolution
Degree to which prototype approximates final product.
Level of detail or sophistication - not just graphic but also functionality.
Scope
Range of what prototype covers.
  • Horizontal prototype: Breadth of features but does not implement them. 
  • Vertical prototype: Selected feature but shows in depth how it works.


Wizard-of-Oz Technique: Faking things that aren’t implemented yet e.g. testing user speech interface, instead of speed detecting, someone is typing the response.
  • Early feedback with incomplete prototypes.
  • More ‘real’ than paper prototyping.
  • Places user at centre of development.
  • Designers learn by playing wizards.
  • Can envision applications that are challenging to build.
  • Low-fidelity: Usually no need for wizard to be unseen.
  • High-fidelity: Create illusion of intelligence interface.


Video Prototypes
  • Cheap, fast and fun - usually shot in one take, avoiding editing.
  • Achieve common ground in design team and with stakeholders.
  • Focuses interface design on the tasks i.e. only things to complete task. 
  • Serves as a spec for developers and creates an MVP.
