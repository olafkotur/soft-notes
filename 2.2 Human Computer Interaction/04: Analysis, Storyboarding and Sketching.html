Storyboards
  • Common mistake is that you focus on the user interface before you focus on the task that the interface is going to support.
  • Storyboarding is all about tasks conveying what people want to achieve.
  • Illustrates problems experienced with current situation or improve designs by using new designs or technology. 


Setting
  • The people involved
  • The environment and situation
  • The task being accomplished
Sequence
  • What steps are involved
  • What is the role of system supporting the task
  • What leads someone to use system
Satisfaction
  • What motivates people to use this system
  • What does it enable them to accomplish
  • What user need does it address

Benefits of Storyboards
  1. Great for getting all stakeholders on same page in terms of goal.
  2. Holistic focus: how user interface helps accomplish task end-to-end.
  3. Avoids commitment to particular interface, describes role of interface and key steps. Means we don’t have to commit to specific details e.g. green button.

Task Analysis
  • Critical way of understanding how users currently do something as well as identifying main points and opportunities for improvement.
  • Process of analysing way people perform their jobs; the things they do, the things they act on and the things they need to know.
  • Sub-tasks: Very much like indentation.
  • Document using task flow i.e. what every part of system needs to do to perform a certain task (user, system, objects). 
What to consider in analysis?
Ignition Point / Trigger
What is it that prompts user to begin task?
Focus Point / Desired outcome
What will tell the user that the task is finished?
Base knowledge
What knowledge does the average user have when they begin the task?
Required Knowledge
What additional knowledge will the user require to reach focus point?
Artefacts
Which tools, processes or information will the user use during the completion of the task?

Ideation Process
  1. Elaborate: Generate solutions to problems which are opportunities.
  2. Reduce: Decide on the ideas worth pursuing.
  3. Repeat: Elaborate and reduce again those solutions.


Sketching
  • Quick and inexpensive: Sketches can be created rapidly at low cost.
  • Disposable: Value is in what it represents not in the sketch itself.
  • Plentiful: Sketches are rarely one-offs, they come in collections that tell story or explore variants of an idea.
  • Exploratory: Sketches suggest and explore ideas rather than define.
  • Ambiguous: Invites interoperation and further ideation.
  • Minimal: Reduced to essence of an idea or concept.
